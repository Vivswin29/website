My Financial Toolkit to MIT

by John Karthick Arul Wincy

Why MIT?

MIT is my dream school where I aim to study Biomedical Engineering with a focus on Neuroscience. But dreams require planning—especially financial planning.

  • Passport & Student Visa (F-1)
  • Tuition & Health Insurance
  • Flight & Travel Costs
  • Housing & Dining Plan
  • Books, Laptop, and Lab Equipment
  • Emergency Savings Fund

Words That Changed My Financial Mindset

“Do not save what is left after spending, but spend what is left after saving.” – Warren Buffett
“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world.” – Nelson Mandela
“Borrow only what you need. Treat your student loan like it’s real money—because it is.” – Financial Consumer Agency of Canada

My Action Plan

  1. Start Saving Aggressively: Opened a high-interest student savings account and saving from freelance work. Goal: $10,000 by Grade 12.
  2. Apply for Scholarships: Targeting Loran, Schulich, TD, MIT Aid. Plan: 20+ applications.
  3. Build Income Streams: Tutoring, coding, blogging, entrepreneurial MIT-style hustle!

Final Thoughts

To be honest, planning for MIT feels kind of overwhelming sometimes. It’s a lot of money, and there’s a lot I need to prepare for. But I’ve learned that with the right mindset and a clear plan, it’s possible. This financial toolkit helped me really think about my future and take control of it instead of just hoping everything works out. I’m not just dreaming about MIT anymore — I’m working toward it, step by step. I know it won’t be easy, but I’m ready to grind for it. And if I can do it, anyone can. - John Karthick
